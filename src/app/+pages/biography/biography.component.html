BIOGRAPHY

Tara Pirayandeh was born on September 21, 2002, in Isfahan, Iran

Tara Pirayandeh has been engaged in art, music, and literature from an early age. At just five, she began her musical journey and quickly mastered the "Tombak". Influenced by prominent artists like Jalil Shahnaz , Mohammadreza Lotfi , Hooshang Kamkar and Pashang Kamkar , she transitioned to the "Tar" in 2011.

Her studies in this traditional Persian instrument have been guided by renowned mentors including Hossein Alizadeh . Tara’s extensive training includes workshops with celebrated musicians such as Bahman Rajabi , Shahram Miljalali , Ali Jahandar , Ali Asghar Shahzaidi and Amir Eslami. She has performed at numerous festivals and concerts both in Iran and internationally, showcasing her exceptional talent.

A prodigy with an extraordinary intellect, Tara accelerated her education, graduating from high school at the age of 13. As a member of Iran's National Elites Foundation, she has actively participated in charitable endeavors, including supporting children with cancer and donating her awards to this cause. Her commitment extends to charity concerts across various Iranian cities

At 17, Tara earned her bachelor’s degree in Computer Science and subsequently received a scholarship to study at Berklee College of Music in Boston, Massachusetts. There, she is pursuing degrees in Film Scoring and Electronic Music Production and Design. Her notable performances include a Norouz celebration in Washington, D.C., for VOA channel

Tara Pirayandeh is dedicated to expanding the reach of Persian and traditional music globally, aiming to introduce and enrich audiences with the cultural heritage of her homeland through her innovative work.
